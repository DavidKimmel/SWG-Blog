The Letter of James

Written by James, the brother of Jesus, this letter is one of the most practical books in the New Testament. Addressed to early Jewish Christians scattered throughout the Roman world, James challenges believers to live out their faith through action — not merely profess it with words.

Across five chapters, James confronts the gap between belief and behavior. He writes about enduring trials with joy, the dangers of an untamed tongue, the call to care for the poor, and the power of patient prayer. His letter reads less like theology and more like wisdom — sharp, direct, and deeply rooted in the teachings of Jesus.

"But be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves." — James 1:22 ESV
