Chapter Four

Submit
to God

"Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you." — James 4:7

James 4:1–17

The Root of Quarrels

James diagnoses the source of conflict with disarming directness: wars and fights among you come from the passions that are at war within you. Coveting, wanting, and not having — then fighting and quarreling to obtain it. The problem is not circumstances but desire. And yet, James notes, you do not have because you do not ask. Even when you ask, you ask with wrong motives, to spend on your own pleasures.

"You desire and do not have, so you murder. You covet and cannot obtain, so you fight and quarrel. You do not have, because you do not ask." — James 4:2 ESV

Friendship With the World

James uses striking language to describe spiritual adultery — friendship with the world as enmity toward God. He is not condemning engagement with culture, but the posture of the heart that chases worldly approval and comfort as its highest good. God jealously yearns for the spirit he has placed within us. The remedy is not self-improvement but humility: draw near to God, and he will draw near to you.

"God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble." — James 4:6 ESV

Planning Without God

The chapter closes with a warning to those who make confident plans — "today or tomorrow we will go into such a town and trade and make a profit" — without acknowledging that life itself is a vapor. This is not a prohibition on planning, but a call to hold plans with open hands, saying instead: "If the Lord wills, we will live and do this or that." To know the right thing to do and not do it is sin.
